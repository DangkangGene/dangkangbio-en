### ARTICLE 3: DEFINITION OF CONFIDENTIAL INFORMATION

**3.1 Definition.** For purposes of this Agreement, "Confidential Information" means any and all information or data, whether oral, written, electronic, or visual, that is disclosed by one Party (the "Disclosing Party") to the other Party (the "Receiving Party"), including but not limited to:

(a) **Technical Data:** Scientific data, research findings, clinical trial results, laboratory protocols, manufacturing processes, formulations, product specifications, quality control procedures, testing methods, and technical know-how related to RPA (Recombinase Polymerase Amplification) molecular detection technology, reagent kits, testing instruments, and Cas enzymes;

(b) **Business Information:** Business plans, strategies, marketing plans, customer lists, supplier information, pricing structures, financial projections, sales data, inventory information, and distribution channel information;

(c) **Intellectual Property:** Patent applications, trademarks, trade secrets, source code, algorithms, proprietary methodologies, and documentation thereof;

(d) **Commercial Information:** Contractual terms with third parties, negotiation strategies, partnership proposals, and commercial terms;

(e) **Regulatory Information:** TGA registration strategies, compliance documentation, regulatory submissions, and approval processes;

(f) **Any other information** that the Disclosing Party designates as confidential or that the Receiving Party knows or reasonably should know is confidential.

**3.2 Form of Disclosure.** Confidential Information may be disclosed in various forms including, but not limited to:
- Written documents
- Oral presentations
- Electronic communications
- Visual presentations or demonstrations
- Product samples or prototypes
- Tangible materials

### ARTICLE 4: EXCLUSIONS FROM CONFIDENTIAL INFORMATION

**4.1 General Exclusions.** The term "Confidential Information" shall not include information that:

(a) **Public Domain:** Is or becomes generally available to the public through no fault of or breach by the Receiving Party;

(b) **Prior Knowledge:** Was known to the Receiving Party prior to disclosure by the Disclosing Party, as evidenced by written records predating the Effective Date;

(c) **Independent Development:** Is independently developed by the Receiving Party without use of or reference to the Disclosing Party's Confidential Information, as evidenced by written records;

(d) **Lawful Receipt:** Is received by the Receiving Party from a third party who has the legal right to disclose it and who does not impose any confidentiality obligation;

(e) **Required Disclosure:** Is required to be disclosed by law, regulation, court order, or governmental authority, provided that the Receiving Party:
- Gives the Disclosing Party prompt written notice of such requirement;
- Cooperates with the Disclosing Party in seeking a protective order or other appropriate remedy; and
- Discloses only that portion of information that is legally required to be disclosed.

**4.2 Burden of Proof.** The Receiving Party bears the burden of establishing that any information falls within the above exclusions.

---

### ARTICLE 5: OBLIGATIONS OF RECEIVING PARTY

**5.1 Non-Disclosure Obligation.** The Receiving Party agrees to:

(a) Hold all Confidential Information of the Disclosing Party in strict confidence;

(b) Not disclose any Confidential Information to any third party without the prior written consent of the Disclosing Party;

(c) Use the Confidential Information solely for the Purpose set forth in Article 2 (the "Purpose");

(d) Limit access to Confidential Information to those employees, officers, directors, contractors, agents, and advisors (collectively, "Representatives") who:
- Have a need to know such information for the Purpose;
- Are bound by confidentiality obligations at least as restrictive as those in this Agreement; and
- Have been informed of the confidential nature of such information.

**5.2 Protection Standards.** The Receiving Party shall:

(a) Protect the Confidential Information using the same degree of care it uses to protect its own confidential information of similar nature, but in no event less than reasonable care;

(b) Implement appropriate administrative, technical, and physical safeguards to prevent unauthorized access, use, or disclosure of Confidential Information;

(c) Promptly notify the Disclosing Party in writing of any unauthorized access, use, or disclosure of Confidential Information;

(d) Assist the Disclosing Party in investigating and remedying any such unauthorized access, use, or disclosure.

**5.3 Limited Use.** The Receiving Party shall not:

(a) Use any Confidential Information for any purpose other than the Purpose;

(b) Copy or reproduce Confidential Information except as reasonably necessary for the Purpose;

(c) Reverse engineer, decompile, or disassemble any products or materials provided as Confidential Information.

### ARTICLE 7: RETURN OR DESTRUCTION OF CONFIDENTIAL INFORMATION

(a) Return to the Disclosing Party all tangible materials containing Confidential Information, including all copies, summaries, and derivative works; or
