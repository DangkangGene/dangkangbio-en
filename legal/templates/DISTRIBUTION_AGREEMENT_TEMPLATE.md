## ARTICLE 5: DISTRIBUTOR OBLIGATIONS

**5.1 Minimum Purchase Commitment.** Distributor shall purchase from Supplier, during each Contract Year, Products with an aggregate value of not less than the amount specified in Schedule D.

**5.2 Sales Efforts.** Distributor shall:

(a) Use its best efforts to promote, market, and sell the Products in the Territory;

(b) Maintain a competent and adequately trained sales force;

(c) Develop and implement marketing and sales plans, subject to Supplier's prior approval for marketing materials;

(d) Attend trade shows, conferences, and other industry events as reasonably requested;

(e) Report to Supplier on market conditions, competitor activities, and customer feedback.

**5.3 Regulatory Compliance.** Distributor shall:

(a) Obtain and maintain all necessary approvals, licenses, and permits required to distribute the Products in the Territory, including TGA registration if required;

(b) Comply with all applicable Australian laws, regulations, and standards, including but not limited to:
- Therapeutic Goods Act 1989 (Cth)
- Therapeutic Goods (Medical Devices) Regulations 2002
- Australian Consumer Law
- Privacy Act 1988 (Cth)
- Work Health and Safety Act 2011 (Cth)

(c) Not make any regulatory submissions or applications relating to the Products without Supplier's prior written consent;

(d) Maintain records of all regulatory submissions and approvals;

(e) Notify Supplier promptly of any regulatory issues, inspections, or adverse events relating to the Products.

**5.4 TGA Registration Support.** Distributor shall:

(a) Assist Supplier in obtaining and maintaining TGA inclusion or registration for the Products;

(b) Provide all necessary information, documentation, and support for TGA applications;

(c) Bear the costs associated with TGA registration, unless otherwise agreed in writing;

(d) Comply with all conditions of TGA registration.

**5.5 Storage and Handling.** Distributor shall:

(a) Store all Products in accordance with the storage conditions specified in the Technical Documentation, including temperature requirements (typically 2-8°C for reagents, or as specified);

(b) Maintain a cold chain management system for temperature-sensitive Products;

(c) Conduct regular inventory checks and quality control inspections;

(d) Not sell or distribute Products that have exceeded their shelf life or have been stored improperly;

(e) Maintain records of storage conditions and inventory management.

**5.6 Packaging and Labeling.** Distributor shall:

(a) Ensure that all Products sold in the Territory comply with Australian labeling requirements;

(b) Not remove, alter, or obscure any labels, markings, or identification on the Products;

(c) Affix additional labels or markings as required by Australian law, provided such additions do not obscure Supplier's original labels;

(d) Use Supplier-approved packaging materials.

**5.7 Reporting Requirements.** Distributor shall provide Supplier with:

(a) **Monthly Sales Reports:** Detailed sales data by product, region, and customer segment, within [fifteen (15)] days following each calendar month;

(b) **Inventory Reports:** Current inventory levels and forecasts, within [ten (10)] days following each calendar month;

(c) **Market Feedback Reports:** Quarterly reports on market trends, competitor activities, and customer satisfaction;

(d) **Regulatory Reports:** Immediate notification of any adverse events, product complaints, or regulatory issues;

(e) **Annual Business Review:** Comprehensive review of sales performance, market development, and plans for the following year.

**5.8 Customer Service.** Distributor shall:

(a) Provide adequate customer support, including technical assistance and product inquiries;

(b) Handle customer complaints in accordance with procedures approved by Supplier;

(c) Forward to Supplier any complaints that involve product quality or safety.

**5.9 No Competition.** During the term of this Agreement, Distributor shall not, without Supplier's prior written consent:

(a) Distribute any products that compete directly with the Products in the Territory;

(b) Represent any competing manufacturers or suppliers of similar products.

## ARTICLE 15: FORCE MAJEURE

**15.1 Definition.** Neither Party shall be liable for any failure or delay in performing its obligations under this Agreement if such failure or delay results from circumstances beyond the reasonable control of that Party, including:

(a) Natural disasters, earthquakes, floods, or acts of God;

(b) War, terrorism, civil unrest, or armed conflict;

(c) Government actions, embargoes, or sanctions;

(d) Pandemics, epidemics, or public health emergencies;

(e) General shortage of materials or labor;

(f) Power failures, telecommunications disruptions, or cyber attacks.

**15.2 Notice and Mitigation.** A Party claiming force majeure shall:

(a) Promptly notify the other Party in writing;

(b) Use reasonable efforts to mitigate the effects of the force majeure event;

(c) Resume performance as soon as reasonably practicable.

**15.3 Extended Force Majeure.** If a force majeure event continues for more than [ninety (90)] days, either Party may terminate this Agreement upon written notice.
