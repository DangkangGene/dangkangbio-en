## ARTICLE 8: PRODUCT RECALL

**8.1 Recall Classification.** Product recalls shall be classified as:

(a) **Class I Recall:** Products that may cause serious adverse health consequences or death;

(b) **Class II Recall:** Products that may cause temporary or reversible adverse health consequences;

(c) **Class III Recall:** Products that are unlikely to cause adverse health consequences.

**8.2 Initiating a Recall.**

(a) **Seller-Initiated Recall:** If Seller determines that a recall is necessary, Seller shall:

   - Notify Buyer within [twenty-four (24)] hours of the recall decision;
   - Provide recall instructions and procedures;
   - Reimburse Buyer for reasonable recall costs.

(b) **Regulatory-Initiated Recall:** If a regulatory authority orders a recall:

   - Buyer shall notify Seller immediately upon receipt of the order;
   - The Parties shall cooperate in implementing the recall.

**8.3 Buyer's Obligations.** Upon notice of a recall:

(a) Buyer shall immediately cease distribution of affected Products;

(b) Buyer shall quarantine and segregate all affected Products;

(c) Buyer shall implement recall procedures in accordance with Seller's instructions;

(d) Buyer shall provide Seller with regular status reports;

(e) Buyer shall cooperate with any regulatory investigations.

**8.4 Costs.** Costs of a recall shall be allocated as follows:

(a) If the recall results from Seller's negligence or breach of warranty, Seller shall bear all costs;

(b) If the recall results from Buyer's negligence or improper handling, Buyer shall bear all costs;

(c) If responsibility cannot be determined, costs shall be shared equally.

**8.5 Regulatory Reporting.** Buyer shall be responsible for all regulatory reporting requirements in the Territory, including reports to TGA. Seller shall provide Buyer with all necessary information and cooperation.
