## 1. Overview of Australian IVD Regulatory Framework

### 1.1 Regulatory Authority

In Australia, in vitro diagnostic medical devices (IVDs) are regulated by the **Therapeutic Goods Administration (TGA)**, a division of the Australian Government Department of Health and Aged Care. The TGA is responsible for evaluating, assessing, and monitoring therapeutic goods including IVDs.

**TGA Mission:**
"To regulate therapeutic goods to protect the health and safety of the Australian community"

**Key Responsibilities:**
- Premarket evaluation of high-risk IVDs
- Post-market surveillance and compliance monitoring
- Conformity assessment of manufacturers
- Establishment and maintenance of the ARTG

### 1.2 Legislative Framework

The Australian IVD regulatory framework is built on:

**Primary Legislation:**
- **Therapeutic Goods Act 1989** - Primary legislation governing therapeutic goods in Australia
- **Therapeutic Goods Regulations 1990** - Detailed regulatory requirements

**Supporting Framework:**
- **Australian Regulatory Guidelines for In Vitro Diagnostic Medical Devices (ARGIVD)**
- **Essential Principles for the Safety and Performance of IVDs**
- **IVD Classification Rules** (Schedule 2A of the Regulations)

### 1.3 Key Regulatory Concepts

**ARTG (Australian Register of Therapeutic Goods):**
All IVDs intended for supply in Australia must be included in the ARTG, except:
- Exempt IVDs (specified in the Regulations)
- Excluded IVDs (not covered by the regulatory scheme)

**Conformity Assessment:**
Process by which a manufacturer demonstrates their IVD meets the essential principles and is safe and performs as intended.

**Sponsor:**
The Australian-licensed person or company responsible for:
- Supplying the IVD in Australia
- TGA communications
- Ongoing compliance obligations

## 2. IVD Classification System

### 2.1 Classification Rules Overview

Australia uses a **risk-based classification system** with four classes, determined by:
- Intended purpose of the IVD
- Clinical significance of the test results
- Potential consequences of incorrect results
- Whether the IVD is intended for self-testing

### 2.2 Classification Definitions

| Class | Risk Level | Description | Examples | Assessment Pathway |
|-------|------------|-------------|----------|-------------------|
| **Class 1** | Low | Self-assessable IVDs with no significant risk | Sample collection devices, basic laboratory reagents | Manufacturer self-declaration |
| **Class 2** | Low-Moderate | IVDs where incorrect results could lead to inappropriate treatment with minor health consequences | Clinical chemistry analyzers, some infection detection tests | QMS audit + technical file |
| **Class 3** | Moderate-High | IVDs where incorrect results could seriously affect patient management or public health | Infectious disease detection, companion diagnostics | Full technical assessment |
| **Class 4** | High | Highest risk IVDs including donor screening and critical disease detection | Blood banking tests, HIV detection, cancer biomarkers | Most stringent assessment |

### 2.3 Classification Rule Details

**Rule 1 - Self-Testing IVDs:**
- All self-testing IVDs are Class 2, 3, or 4 depending on clinical consequence

**Rule 2 - Infectious Disease Testing:**
- Detection of markers for serious infectious diseases: **Class 4**
- Detection of other infectious disease markers: **Class 3**

**Rule 3 - Companion Diagnostics:**
- All companion diagnostics are **Class 3**

**Rule 4 - Cervical Cancer Screening:**
- HPV testing for primary screening: **Class 3**

**Rule 5 - Genetic Testing:**
- Testing for high-risk genetic mutations: **Class 3**
- Carrier testing for genetic disorders: **Class 3**

**Rule 6 - Blood/Donor Screening:**
- Screening of donated blood, tissue, or cells: **Class 4**

**Rule 7 - Critical Care Testing:**
- Near-patient tests for critical parameters: **Class 3**

**Rule 8 - Anatomical Pathology:**
- Detection of precancerous conditions: **Class 3**

**Rule 9 - Other IVDs:**
- Default classification based on intended use and consequences

### 2.4 Dangkang Bio Product Classification Assessment

| Product Category | Likely Classification | Rationale |
|------------------|----------------------|-----------|
| Animal disease detection kits (ASF, WSSV, etc.) | Class 2 or 3 | Depends on clinical consequence and species |
| Food safety pathogen kits | Class 2 | Generally lower individual health consequence |
| Point-of-care infectious disease tests | Class 2-3 | Based on specific pathogen |
| Research use only reagents | Not applicable | If labeled and supplied as RUO |
| Instrumentation (ELSN16, ERCF8) | Class 1 (if accessories) / Class 2 (if standalone) | Depends on intended use claim |

## 3. Conformity Assessment Requirements

### 3.1 Assessment Pathways by Class

**Class 1 IVDs:**
- **Pathway:** Self-declaration of conformity
- **Requirements:** 
  - Declaration of conformity by manufacturer
  - Basic labeling compliance
  - No TGA conformity assessment required
- **Process:** Include in ARTG based on self-assessment

**Class 2 IVDs:**
- **Pathway:** Audit-based conformity assessment
- **Requirements:**
  - Quality Management System (ISO 13485)
  - Technical file review
  - Limited audit of manufacturing processes
- **Process:** TGA reviews technical documentation and conducts audit

**Class 3 IVDs:**
- **Pathway:** Full conformity assessment
- **Requirements:**
  - ISO 13485 QMS
  - Comprehensive technical file
  - Full audit of design and manufacturing
  - Clinical performance data
- **Process:** Detailed TGA evaluation including design dossier examination

**Class 4 IVDs:**
- **Pathway:** Most stringent assessment
- **Requirements:**
  - ISO 13485 QMS
  - Complete design dossier
  - Batch release testing protocols
  - Blood/tissue safety considerations
- **Process:** TGA conducts extensive evaluation with expert panel input

### 3.2 Quality Management System Requirements

**ISO 13485:2016 Compliance:**

The foundation of IVD regulatory compliance in Australia is a certified Quality Management System based on **ISO 13485:2016**. Key requirements include:

**Document Control:**
- Quality manual
- Documented procedures for all critical processes
- Record retention (minimum 10 years for IVDs)

**Design Control (Clause 7.3):**
- Design and development planning
- Design inputs and outputs
- Design review, verification, and validation
- Design transfer
- Design history file (DHF)

**Purchasing Controls:**
- Supplier qualification
- Purchased product verification
- Critical supplier monitoring

**Production Controls:**
- Manufacturing procedures
- Equipment qualification and calibration
- Process validation
- Environmental controls
- Contamination prevention

**Traceability:**
- Component traceability
- Batch/lot traceability
- Distribution tracking

**CAPA System:**
- Complaint handling
- Investigation procedures
- Corrective and preventive actions
- Management review

### 3.3 Risk Management Requirements

**ISO 14971:2019 Compliance:**

Risk management must be integrated throughout the product lifecycle:

**Risk Analysis:**
- Intended use and reasonably foreseeable misuse
- Hazard identification
- Risk estimation

**Risk Evaluation:**
- Risk criteria
- Risk acceptability determination

**Risk Control:**
- Mitigation measures
- Residual risk assessment
- Risk-benefit analysis

**Overall Risk Assessment:**
- Completeness check
- Post-market risk monitoring
- Periodic review

**Required Documentation:**
- Risk management plan
- Risk management file
- Risk analysis report
- Benefit-risk analysis

### 3.4 Performance Evaluation Requirements

**Clinical Evidence:**

All IVDs require clinical evidence supporting their performance claims:

**Performance Characteristics:**
- Analytical sensitivity (Limit of Detection)
- Analytical specificity (cross-reactivity)
- Precision (repeatability and reproducibility)
- Accuracy (trueness and bias)
- Measuring range
- Cut-off determination

**Clinical Performance:**
- Clinical sensitivity
- Clinical specificity
- Positive predictive value (PPV)
- Negative predictive value (NPV)
- Agreement studies

**Evidence Sources:**
- Published literature
- In-house performance evaluation studies
- Published proficiency testing data
- Reference method comparison

## 4. Australian Sponsor Requirements

### 4.1 Mandatory Sponsor Requirement

**Critical:** All IVDs supplied in Australia must have a registered Australian sponsor. The sponsor is:

- Legally responsible for the IVD in Australia
- The point of contact for TGA communications
- Responsible for ensuring ongoing compliance
- Accountable for adverse event reporting

### 4.2 Sponsor Responsibilities

**Pre-Market:**
- Engage TGA conformity assessment body (if required)
- Submit ARTG inclusion application
- Ensure all documentation is complete and compliant

**Ongoing:**
- Maintain TGA registration
- Coordinate with manufacturer on compliance matters
- Report adverse events to TGA (within prescribed timeframes)
- Implement recall actions if required
- Pay annual ARTG inclusion fees
- Notify TGA of changes to registered information

### 4.3 Selecting an Australian Sponsor

**Options for International Manufacturers:**

1. **Appoint a dedicated Australian distributor** as sponsor
2. **Establish an Australian subsidiary** to act as sponsor
3. **Engage a regulatory consultant** or authorized representative to act as sponsor
4. **Partner with an Australian company** experienced in IVD regulatory affairs

**Evaluation Criteria:**
- Experience with TGA regulatory processes
- Understanding of IVD classification and requirements
- Infrastructure for adverse event reporting
- Capacity for ongoing compliance monitoring
- Financial stability and longevity

### 4.4 TGA Business Services (TBS)

All sponsor interactions with TGA occur through the **TGA Business Services** portal:
- Online portal for all applications
- Document submission
- Fee payments
- Communication tracking

## 5. In-House IVD Requirements

### 5.1 In-House IVD Definition

In-house IVDs are IVDs that are manufactured and used within a single laboratory or healthcare facility, typically for patient testing purposes. They are not supplied commercially but are used within the institution.

### 5.2 Classification-Based Requirements

**Class 1-3 In-House IVDs:**

Requirements for laboratories using in-house Class 1-3 IVDs:
- **NATA Accreditation:** Laboratory must hold NATA accreditation against ISO 15189
- **NPAAC Compliance:** Must comply with National Pathology Accreditation Advisory Council standards
- **TGA Notification:** Must notify TGA annually (by July 1) of in-house IVD use
- **Performance Verification:** Internal validation required
- **Quality Control:** Ongoing QC procedures mandatory

**Class 4 In-House IVDs:**
- Must be included in the ARTG (cannot rely on in-house exemption)
- Same requirements as commercial Class 4 IVDs

### 5.3 NATA Accreditation

The **National Association of Testing Authorities (NATA)** provides accreditation for medical testing laboratories in Australia.

**Accreditation Standards:**
- ISO 15189:2022 - Medical laboratories - Requirements for quality and competence
- NPAAC Standards (as applicable)

**Benefits of NATA Accreditation:**
- Demonstrates technical competence
- Required for Medicare/PBS rebate claims
- Enables use of in-house IVDs
- Enhances credibility with referring practitioners

### 5.4 TGA Notification Process

**Annual Notification Requirements:**
- List of in-house IVDs used
- Confirmation of NATA accreditation
- Statement of compliance with NPAAC requirements
- Any significant changes to testing repertoire

**Notification Timeline:**
- Due annually by **July 1**
- Submitted via TGA Business Services portal
- Applies to previous 12-month period

### 6.2 Detailed Step Descriptions

**STEP 1: Determine IVD Classification**
- Review Schedule 2A classification rules
- Document intended use claims
- Determine appropriate class for each product
- Consider worst-case classification scenario

**STEP 2: Prepare Technical Documentation**
- Compile design history file
- Prepare risk management documentation (ISO 14971)
- Gather performance evaluation data
- Draft labeling and instructions for use
- Ensure all documentation meets TGA requirements

**STEP 3: Establish Quality Management System**
- Gap analysis against ISO 13485:2016
- Implement necessary procedures and controls
- Conduct internal quality audits
- Engage certification body for ISO 13485 certification (required for Class 2-4)

**STEP 4: Engage Australian Sponsor**
- Identify sponsor candidates
- Evaluate experience and capability
- Negotiate agreement terms
- Ensure sponsor understands product and regulatory requirements

**STEP 5: Submit TGA Application**
- Complete application forms via TGA Business Services
- Compile application dossier
- Pay application fees
- Ensure sponsor submits on your behalf

**STEP 6: TGA Assessment**
- Respond to any information requests
- Participate in QMS audit if scheduled
- Address non-conformities identified
- Provide additional documentation as requested

**STEP 7: ARTG Inclusion**
- Receive inclusion notification
- Pay annual ARTG inclusion fee
- Update labeling with ARTG number
- Commence supply (for approved applications)

**STEP 8: Post-Market Compliance**
- Implement post-market surveillance system
- Establish adverse event monitoring
- Maintain QMS certification
- Report changes to TGA before implementation
- Pay annual fees on time

| **Class 2** | 3-6 months | QMS audit scheduling; technical file completeness |
| **Class 3** | 6-12 months | Detailed assessment; potential additional data requirements |
| **Class 4** | 12-18+ months | Comprehensive review; expert panel consultations |

## 7. Required Documentation

### 7.1 Technical File Contents

**For Class 2-4 IVDs, the technical file must include:**

**A. Device Description and Specification:**
- Complete description of the IVD
- Intended use and indications
- Principles of operation
- Design specifications
- Component and accessory lists
- Software specifications (if applicable)

**B. Design and Manufacturing Information:**
- Design history file (DHF)
- Manufacturing process descriptions
- Manufacturing site information
- Sterilization methods (if applicable)
- Critical supplier list

**C. Risk Management Documentation:**
- Risk management plan
- Risk analysis report
- Risk evaluation records
- Risk control measures
- Residual risk assessment
- Benefit-risk analysis
- Risk management report

**D. Performance Evaluation:**
- Analytical performance data
  - Sensitivity (LoD)
  - Specificity (cross-reactivity)
  - Precision
  - Accuracy
  - Measuring range
  - Hook effects (for immunoassays)
- Clinical performance data
  - Clinical sensitivity
  - Clinical specificity
  - PPV and NPV
  - Agreement studies
- Literature review (if used as evidence)

**E. Labeling:**
- All labeling samples
- Instructions for use (IFU)
- Package inserts
- Quick reference guides
- Electronic labeling (if applicable)

**F. Quality Management:**
- QMS certificate (ISO 13485)
- Audit reports
- CAPA records
- Complaint records
- Customer feedback

**G. Post-Market Surveillance:**
- Post-market surveillance plan
- Vigilance reporting procedures
- Trend reporting system
- Periodic safety update reports (PSURs)

### 7.2 Common Technical Deficiencies

**TGA commonly identifies issues with:**
- Incomplete risk analysis
- Insufficient clinical performance data
- Inadequate validation of claimed performance
- Labeling not meeting requirements
- Software documentation gaps
- Sterilization validation issues

## 8. Companion Diagnostics (CDx)

### 8.1 Australian CDx Definition

A companion diagnostic is an IVD that is essential for the safe and effective use of a corresponding therapeutic product. Examples include:
- HER2 testing for Herceptin (trastuzumab)
- EGFR testing for targeted cancer therapies
- PD-L1 testing for immunotherapy selection

### 8.2 CDx Classification

**All companion diagnostics are classified as Class 3 IVDs**, regardless of other classification rules.

### 8.3 CDx Requirements

**Separate ARTG Entry:**
- Companion diagnostic requires its own ARTG inclusion
- Cannot rely on therapeutic product approval

**Companion Testing Plan:**
- Documentation of the relationship with the therapeutic product
- Intended use statement linking to specific therapy
- Clinical evidence supporting companion use

**Parallel Development Considerations:**
- Coordinate with therapeutic product developer
- Align regulatory timelines where possible
- Consider concurrent submission strategies

### 8.4 Implications for Dangkang Bio

If Dangkang Bio develops tests intended to guide therapeutic decisions:
- All such tests will be Class 3 IVDs
- More comprehensive clinical evidence required
- Higher regulatory burden and costs
- Consider business case carefully

## 11. Additional Considerations

### 11.1 State and Territory Variations

While TGA provides national regulation, some areas may have additional requirements:
- **QLD, NSW, VIC:** Some state health departments have additional licensing
- **Import requirements:** Biosecurity clearance may be needed
- **Transport/storage:** Cold chain requirements may trigger additional compliance

### 11.2 Environmental and Sustainability

- **Waste disposal:** IVD waste may require special handling
- **Packaging:** Environmental regulations may apply
- **REACH/RoHS:** Electronic equipment compliance

### 11.3 Data Protection and Privacy

- **Patient data:** If IVD generates patient results, Australian Privacy Act applies
- **Cloud services:** Data storage must comply with Australian data sovereignty requirements
- **Notifiable Data Breaches:** Must have incident response procedures

### 11.4 Customs and Import

- **Import permit:** May be required for certain biological materials
- **Customs clearance:** Proper documentation essential
- **Quarantine:** Biosecurity requirements for biological products
