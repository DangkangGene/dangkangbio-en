## Application Note 1: African Swine Fever (ASF) Detection

### Rapid Field Detection of African Swine Fever Virus Using RPA Technology

---

### Background

African Swine Fever (ASF) is a highly contagious viral disease affecting domestic and wild pigs, caused by the African Swine Fever Virus (ASFV), a large double-stranded DNA virus belonging to the *Asfarviridae* family. ASF causes mortality rates approaching 100% in acute cases and has become a significant global threat to pork production since its spread beyond Africa in 2007.

**Current Global Situation:**
- ASF has spread to more than 50 countries across Asia, Europe, and Africa
- Economic losses estimated in billions of dollars
- No commercially available vaccine (as of 2024)
- Control relies heavily on early detection and rapid culling

**Diagnostic Challenges:**
- Symptoms can be confused with other diseases (e.g., classical swine fever)
- Rapid spread requires fast diagnostic turnaround
- Many affected regions lack sophisticated laboratory infrastructure
- Wildlife reservoirs complicate eradication efforts

### Challenge

A Southeast Asian pork production company with operations across five countries faced significant challenges in ASF monitoring:

**Existing PCR Testing Limitations:**
- Required expensive thermal cyclers ($15,000-30,000 per unit)
- Needed reliable electricity and climate-controlled laboratories
- Turnaround time of 24-48 hours for samples shipped to central labs
- High per-test cost ($25-50 USD) limited testing frequency
- Professional operators required for complex procedures

**Field Conditions:**
- Pig farms located in remote areas
- Limited access to laboratory facilities
- Unreliable power supply
- Need for rapid screening before animal transport
- Large number of samples requiring testing

The company sought a diagnostic solution that could:
1. Provide results within 30 minutes of sample collection
2. Operate with minimal equipment
3. Be performed by farm personnel with basic training
4. Maintain laboratory-equivalent sensitivity and specificity
5. Enable point-of-contact decision making

### Solution: Dangkang Bio RT-RPA Strip Detection Kit

**Implementation:**

The company deployed Dangkang Bio's **RT-RPA Strip Kit for ASFV Detection** across 23 farm sites and 8 border inspection points.

**Sample Processing:**
1. Blood samples collected from live animals (or organ samples from deceased pigs)
2. Nucleic acid extraction using Dangkang Bio's Magnetic Bead Extraction Kit
   - Manual protocol: 10-15 minutes
   - Simplified "rapid extraction" protocol: 5 minutes (buffer-based)
3. Reconstituted lyophilized RPA reagents
4. Amplification: 15 minutes at 39°C using the ERCF8 isothermal detector or dry block heater
5. Lateral flow detection: 3-5 minutes

**Total Time-to-Result:** 25-35 minutes from sample collection

**Equipment Required:**
- Dangkang Bio ERCF8 Isothermal Fluorescence Detector (8-well, battery operated)
- Mini centrifuge
- Vortex mixer
- Basic pipettes
- Portable dry block heater (backup)
- Cooler box for transport

**Training:**
- 2-hour hands-on training for farm managers
- Illustrated quick-reference guide provided
- Video tutorial available via QR code on kit packaging

**Validation Study:**

The company conducted a 6-month comparative study across all sites:

| **Specificity** | 100% | 99.4% | 99.4% NP |
| **Total Samples** | 1,247 | 1,247 | - |

| **False Positives** | 0 | 7 | - |
| **False Negatives** | 0 | 3 | - |

**Key Performance Metrics:**
- **Limit of Detection:** 10 copies/μL (comparable to conventional PCR)
- **Detection Time:** 15-20 minutes (vs. 2 hours for PCR)
- **Time-to-Result:** 30-35 minutes (vs. 24-48 hours including transport)
- **Cost per Test:** $8-12 USD (vs. $25-50 USD for PCR)

**Operational Impact:**

During the study period, ASFV was detected at 4 farm sites:
- **3 sites** were confirmed positive through subsequent laboratory PCR
- **1 site** was a false positive (7 total from RPA, 3 confirmed by PCR)
- All 3 true positives were identified before clinical symptoms appeared in surrounding pens
- Early detection enabled rapid culling and prevented spread to adjacent farms

**Field Usability Assessment:**

### Conclusion

The Dangkang Bio RT-RPA Strip Kit demonstrated:

1. **High Diagnostic Accuracy:** 96.8% positive percent agreement and 99.4% negative percent agreement with laboratory PCR.

2. **Rapid Turnaround:** Results available within 35 minutes compared to 24-48 hours, enabling real-time decision making.

3. **Field Deployment:** Minimal equipment and power requirements allowed deployment to remote locations without laboratory infrastructure.

4. **Cost Reduction:** Per-test cost reduced by approximately 70% when including equipment, transport, and labor savings.

- 40% increase in testing frequency due to reduced costs
- 2 disease outbreaks prevented through early detection
- Estimated annual savings of $450,000 in disease-related losses

## Application Note 2: Food Safety Pathogen Detection

### Rapid Detection of *Salmonella* spp. in Food Products Using RPA Technology

---

### Background

*Salmonella* remains one of the most significant causes of foodborne illness worldwide. The bacteria cause an estimated 1.35 million infections annually in the United States alone, resulting in 26,500 hospitalizations and 420 deaths (CDC data). Contaminated food products including poultry, eggs, meat, fresh produce, and processed foods represent the primary transmission route.

**Regulatory Environment:**
- Many jurisdictions require zero-tolerance policies for *Salmonella* in ready-to-eat products
- Testing is mandatory at multiple points: raw materials, processing, finished products, and environmental monitoring
- Rapid results enable timely release of products, reducing inventory holding costs
- Import/export requirements often specify testing protocols

**Industry Challenges:**
- Traditional culture methods require 3-5 days for definitive results
- ELISA methods require 24-48 hours and may have lower sensitivity
- PCR methods are faster but require expensive equipment and trained operators
- Need for high-throughput testing at multiple production points
- Pressure to release products quickly while ensuring safety

### Challenge

A mid-sized poultry processing company in Australia processed approximately 50,000 birds per day and was required to test:
- Carcass rinses (every 500 birds)
- Environmental swabs (multiple points per shift)
- Finished product samples (random sampling)
- Rejects and suspicious samples (per incident)

**Testing Volume:**
- ~100 samples per day across 3 shifts
- Turnaround time critical for processing line decisions
- Laboratory testing (culture) required 3-5 days, creating significant product holding

**Requirements:**
- Results within 2 hours of sample collection
- High sensitivity (regulatory requirement for low limits of detection)
- High throughput compatible with production pace
- Ability to test multiple sample types
- Equipment suitable for factory floor environment
- Cost-effective for high-volume screening

### Solution: Dangkang Bio RPA-Based Testing Protocol

**Implementation:**

The company implemented a tiered testing strategy:

**Tier 1: High-Throughput Screening (RPA)**
- Dangkang Bio **Salmonella* RPA Strip Kit
- Pre-enrichment: 6-8 hours in buffered peptone water
- RPA amplification: 15 minutes at 39°C
- Lateral flow detection: 3-5 minutes
- Total time: 8-10 hours (including pre-enrichment)

**Tier 2: Confirmatory Testing**
- Positive RPA results confirmed by standard culture method
- Retained for legal documentation
- Environmental re-sampling for positives

**Sample Processing Protocol:**

1. **Sample Collection:**
   - Carcass rinsate: 30 mL collected post-chiller
   - Environmental: sponge swabs in 10 mL BPW
   - Product: 25 g sample in 225 mL BPW

2. **Pre-enrichment:**
   - 37°C incubation for 6-8 hours
   - Shake or static as appropriate

3. **RPA Testing:**
   - 1 mL pre-enriched sample
   - DNA extraction via heat treatment (95°C, 5 minutes)
   - RPA reaction setup (lyophilized reagents pre-loaded)
   - Incubation at 39°C for 15 minutes
   - Lateral flow development: 3-5 minutes

4. **Interpretation:**
   - Two lines (control + test): Positive
   - One line (control only): Negative
   - No control line: Invalid, repeat test

**Equipment:**
- Dry block heater (39°C, accommodates multiple strips)
- Heat block for DNA extraction (95°C)
- Vortex mixer
- Pipettes
- Portable refrigerator for reagent storage

### Results

**Method Validation:**

Before full implementation, the company conducted a comprehensive validation study:

**Comparison with ISO 6579 Culture Method (n=400 samples):**

| Sample Type | n | Sensitivity | Specificity | PPA | NPA |
|-------------|---|-------------|-------------|-----|-----|
| Carcass rinsate | 150 | 100% | 97.8% | 100% | 97.8% |
| Environmental | 120 | 95.5% | 98.9% | 95.5% | 98.9% |
| Chicken meat | 80 | 100% | 98.6% | 100% | 98.6% |
| Feed ingredients | 50 | 100% | 100% | 100% | 100% |
| **Overall** | **400** | **98.8%** | **98.6%** | **98.8%** | **98.6%** |

**Limit of Detection:**
- Method detected 1-5 CFU/25g in spiked samples after 6-hour enrichment
- Equivalent to ISO 6579 sensitivity

**Reproducibility:**
- Within-laboratory CV: <10% (duplicate testing)
- Operator variation: <5% (3 operators tested)
- Lot-to-lot variation: <8% (3 reagent lots)

**Operational Performance (6-Month Pilot):**

|--------|---------------------|-------------|-------------|

**Sample Testing Results (6 months):**

### Conclusion

The Dangkang Bio RPA-based *Salmonella* testing protocol demonstrated:

1. **Equivalent Performance:** 98.8% sensitivity and 98.6% specificity compared to ISO culture method, meeting regulatory requirements.

2. **Significant Time Savings:** Results available within 10 hours compared to 3-5 days for culture, dramatically reducing product holding time.

The company successfully implemented RPA testing as the primary screening tool, retaining culture methods for regulatory confirmations and dispute resolution. The system enabled:
- Same-day product release for most samples
- Identification of a processing equipment sanitation issue (through environmental monitoring)
- Compliance with customer and regulatory requirements
- Estimated annual savings exceeding $1 million AUD

## Application Note 4: Environmental Monitoring

### Detection of *Vibrio parahaemolyticus* in Coastal Waters Using RPA Technology

---

### Background

*Vibrio parahaemolyticus* is a Gram-negative bacterium found naturally in marine and estuarine environments. It is a leading cause of seafood-associated gastroenteritis worldwide, with symptoms including watery diarrhea, abdominal cramping, nausea, vomiting, and fever.

**Significance:**
- Third most common cause of bacterial foodborne illness in Asia
- Increasing prevalence in temperate waters (climate change)
- Associated with consumption of raw or undercooked shellfish
- Can cause wound infections and septicemia in immunocompromised individuals

**Environmental Monitoring Importance:**
- Coastal waters serve as reservoirs for pathogenic strains
- Seafood harvesting areas require regular monitoring
- Post-hurricane or flood events increase contamination risk
- Public health protection through early warning

### Challenge

A seafood safety monitoring program operated by a Pacific island nation's Ministry of Health needed to enhance environmental surveillance capabilities.

**Program Objectives:**
- Monitor 15 designated shellfish harvesting areas
- Conduct monthly water sampling
- Provide results within 24 hours to inform harvesting decisions
- Enable rapid response to contamination events
- Support regulatory compliance with Codex Alimentarius standards

**Existing Constraints:**
- Central laboratory 3 hours from most sampling sites
- Limited cold chain transport capability
- Traditional culture methods (MPN) require 18-24 hours
- Equipment and reagent costs limited budget
- Need for field-deployable testing capability

**Technical Requirements:**
- Detection of *V. parahaemolyticus* at regulatory action levels (>30 MPN/100g shellfish)
- Ability to test water samples directly
- Minimal equipment for field use
- Results comparable to standard culture methods

### Solution: Dangkang Bio Environmental Monitoring Protocol

**Implementation:**

The program implemented a dual approach combining field screening and laboratory confirmation:

**Field Screening (RPA):**
- Dangkang Bio *Vibrio parahaemolyticus* RPA Kit
- Enrichment: 4-6 hours in alkaline peptone water (APW)
- RPA amplification: 15 minutes at 39°C
- Fluorescence detection using ERCF8

**Laboratory Confirmation:**
- Positive RPA results → standard culture confirmation (ISO 21872)
- Negative RPA results → no further action (for screening purposes)

**Field Protocol:**

1. **Sample Collection:**
   - Water samples: 500 mL in sterile bottles
   - Shellfish samples: 10-15 animals, pooled tissue
   - Field blanks and positive controls included

2. **Field Processing:**
   - Samples placed in insulated cooler with ice packs
   - Transported to mobile testing unit (van equipped)
   - Arrival at testing site within 2 hours

3. **Enrichment (Field Laboratory):**
   - 100 mL water + 100 mL double-strength APW
   - OR 10 g shellfish homogenate + 90 mL APW
   - 37°C incubation: 4-6 hours (incubator in van)

4. **RPA Testing:**
   - DNA extraction: Heat lysis (95°C, 10 min)
   - RPA setup: Lyophilized reagents
   - Amplification: 15 minutes at 39°C
   - Detection: Real-time fluorescence (ERCF8)

5. **Result Interpretation:**
   - Ct value <15: Strong positive
   - Ct value 15-20: Positive
   - No Ct: Negative
   - All positives → culture confirmation

**Equipment List (Mobile Unit):**
- Dangkang Bio ERCF8 (battery operated)
- Portable incubator (37°C)
- Dry block heater (95°C)
- Mini centrifuge
- Vortex mixer
- Portable refrigerator
- Power inverter (for vehicle)
- PPE and biosafety containers

### Results

**Method Validation:**

Comparison with ISO 21872 culture method (n=180 environmental samples):

| Sample Type | n | Sensitivity | Specificity | PPA | NPA |
|-------------|---|-------------|-------------|-----|-----|
| Seawater | 80 | 94.1% | 97.1% | 94.1% | 97.1% |
| Estuarine water | 50 | 92.3% | 98.0% | 92.3% | 98.0% |
| Shellfish tissue | 50 | 100% | 100% | 100% | 100% |
| **Overall** | **180** | **95.8%** | **98.2%** | **95.8%** | **98.2%** |

**Limit of Detection:**
- Seawater: 10 CFU/100 mL (after enrichment)
- Shellfish: 30 MPN/100g (regulatory action level achievable)

**Field Performance (18-Month Pilot):**

| Metric | Result |
|--------|--------|
| Total samples tested | 1,247 |
| Samples processed in field | 1,089 (87%) |
